Understanding Peripheral Neuropathy

Peripheral neuropathy involves damage to the nerves outside the brain and spinal cord. These nerves transmit information between your central nervous system and the rest of your body. Understanding this condition is the first step to having informed conversations with healthcare providers.

🔬

Key Statistics

Peripheral neuropathy is more common than many realize, affecting up to 30% of adults over 65. In people with diabetes, the prevalence can reach 50%, making it an important health topic to understand.

🚨

Warning Signs

Gradual onset of numbness and tingling that may spread upward from feet or hands, sharp or burning pain, extreme sensitivity to touch, muscle weakness, and coordination difficulties.

🧩

Contributing Factors

Several factors can contribute to peripheral nerve issues, including metabolic conditions, vitamin deficiencies, toxin exposure, injury, infections, and genetic predispositions.
